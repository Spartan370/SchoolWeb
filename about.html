About Our University

Our History

Founded in 1848, Ole Miss Gladiators University has a rich history of academic excellence and tradition. Located in Oxford, Mississippi, our university has grown from a small college to a major research institution with over 24,500 students.

The university was established by the state legislature as the flagship institution for higher education in Mississippi. Through the Civil War, two World Wars, and the civil rights movement, Ole Miss Gladiators has stood as a beacon of education and progress in the South.

Campus Architecture

Our campus features stunning Greek Revival architecture, characterized by grand columns, symmetrical shapes, and elaborate detailing. The iconic buildings include:

  • Gladiator Hall (1851) - Administrative Building
  • Victory Lyceum (1863) - Liberal Arts
  • Colosseum Library (1889) - Main Library
  • Spartan Science Center (1923) - STEM Facilities

The red brick buildings and white columns create a cohesive, classical appearance that reflects our university's historic roots while providing modern educational facilities.

Our 1,000-acre campus is known for its beautiful landscaping, featuring ancient oak trees, vibrant gardens, and open green spaces where students gather to study and socialize.

Traditions & Pride

Ole Miss Gladiators University is steeped in traditions that foster a strong sense of community and school pride:

The Colosseum Clash

Our annual football game against our biggest rival, where students and alumni dress in full gladiator regalia.

Torch Ceremony

A solemn passing of the torch from seniors to juniors, symbolizing the continuation of knowledge and leadership.

Victory March

After athletic victories, students march from the stadium to the campus center, led by the Gladiator Marching Band.
